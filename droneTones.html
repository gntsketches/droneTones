About the app:

DroneTones plays up to 8 synthesizers by swelling and fading their volumes in a randomized pattern. The pitch of each synth is chosen by interval relative to the root pitch.

Just before each synth volume rises, its tone color is randomly chosen from the selections in "Synth Overtone Options". These settings manipulate the partials of the synth's overtone series (* see below).

Each synth is also assigned a vibrato and auto-filter effect. The rate and depth of these effects are assigned a random value between zero and the chosen settings in "Synth Effects Options".

The randomized timing for the rise and fall of synth volumes, and the rest between, can be adjusted in "Timing". Setting the min above the max causes the max to track accordingly (and vice-versa). Note that running the app too "quickly" (with very fast rise, fall, and rest times) may overload the computer's processor, causing the sound to skip and cut out.

* Options include: a Sawtooth wave (all overtones at decreasing volumes); Full Volumes, a specified number of overtones at maximum volume, Random Volumes, with a number of overtones at randomly chosen volumes (this is only distinct with a small number), and Clusters, with a number of clusters of overtones at random volumes, spread across the overtone series by 'density'. Desktop/laptop users can open the Javascipt console (F12) to see the array of overtones/partials for each new type of synth as they are generated. (Note that logging to the console also is an additional load on the computer's processor, and may cause performance problems if the app is run quickly.)
